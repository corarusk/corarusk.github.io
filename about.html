“Every success story is a tale of constant adaptation, revision, and change.”

Who am I?

My path to a career in UX design has been all but a direct flight. It took many meaningful pit stops and flight changes to get where I am today. Nonetheless, I’m thankful because those “layovers” have molded me to be the person I am today. And who am I?

I am Cora Rusk. A passionate go-getter. Someone driven by the need to understand people and solve their everyday problems. Selfless and willing to take risks.

I am a UX Designer.

AT THE AGE OF 9,

I got my first babysitting gig. Fast forward 3 years and I am the hottest commodity in the babysitting world. I even obtained a Red Cross Babysitting Certification. I take my work seriously, okay? On my off time, I was either rearranging my bedroom or transforming my MySpace profile about every other week. Little did I know, this was the beginning of my love for design and tech.

COLLEGE.

Right off the bat, I knew I wanted to be a teacher. You get an entire classroom to decorate? Choose a new color scheme every year? Summers off? Oh, and did I mention that I really love kids? Seems like the perfect fit… right?

TWO YEARS INTO MY TEACHING CAREER.

I’m established, successful, but feeling… Eh.

Eh (Adjective) Of bad quality.
My french fries were eh.

That’s exactly how I was feeling about my career. Sure, I like education. Actually, I love education. Was I good at it? Sure. But a passion inside of me wasn’t being fueled the way I know it could be. I had a dream and drive to be a part of something bigger. I had no idea what that was. Heck, the only thing I’ve ever been my whole life was a teacher. Could I even be anything else?

THE SOURCE OF MY EPIPHANY.

My husband. A technical recruiter specializing in senior level engineering roles, had a new type of position to fill. Something called a UX Designer? What’s that? As he was learning about the role of a UX designer, he felt that every bullet point he went over screamed:

"CORA WOULD BE SO GOOD AT THIS!"

TL;DR

an ample amount of YouTube and Blog binging helped me discover the field of design. The more I learned, the more I loved.
I am so certain about this career change and so certain that UX would become a part of my identity. So, what does any rational dream-chaser do? I quit my full-time job as a teacher and enrolled in Bloc’s UX Design Apprenticeship program. In other words, I made the best decision of my life.

A CAREER CHANGE IS HARD WORK.

It takes discipline, especially being self-paced and online. Through the program and guidance from my mentor, Becky Olsen, I learned how to reposition my previously acquired skills and channel them to produce user centered products. UX design encompasses all of my passions in one.
Design is more than just a pretty face, it is a real solution to someone’s real problem. It’s a part of who I am.
